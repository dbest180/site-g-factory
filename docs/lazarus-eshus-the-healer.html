Lazarus: The Healer Who Walked Unbound

I found him in a small village on the edge of a dying empire. A plague had swept through, leaving children orphaned and elders gasping for breath. The local healers had given up. But Eshua moved among the dying with a quiet certainty I had not seen since Bethany.

He smiled. "And you are Lazarus. The one who never died."

I should not have been surprised. Immortality grants perspective, not omniscience. But he knew me‚Äînot as legend, but as I was. He saw through the centuries I carried like stones in my pockets.

I warned him. "They will kill you."

He nodded. "I know."

"And you'll let them?"

"If that is what it takes."

I did not understand then. After two thousand years of watching death, I thought I understood everything about it. But Eshua's death was unlike any I had witnessed.
